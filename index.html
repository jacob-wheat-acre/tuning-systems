What you're seeing

A Lissajous figure plots x(t) = sin(2πf₁t) against y(t) = sin(2πf₂t). When f₁/f₂ is a simple integer ratio — like 3:2 in just intonation — the curve closes into a stable geometric shape. In equal temperament the ratio is irrational, so the figure slowly precesses: it never quite repeats.

Try switching between tunings on the Perfect 5th: just and Pythagorean give a locked figure; equal temperament slowly rotates.
